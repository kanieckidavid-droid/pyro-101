⚠ Start here — staying safe & legal

  • Fireworks are explosives. A sober shooter, eye and ear protection, and a charged hose or fire extinguisher within reach are non-negotiable.
  • Know your class: 1.4G is consumer-grade; 1.3G is professional display-grade and generally requires a license. If you're unsure which you have, treat it as the more restricted class.
  • Check local laws and permits before you buy or fire — they vary by state, county, and town.
  • Keep your distance: a widely used minimum is at least 70 ft of clearance for every inch of shell diameter — treat that as a floor, give yourself more whenever you can, and keep spectators well back and upwind.
  • Never lean over a tube, and never return to a dud — wait at least 20 minutes (longer is safer), then soak it thoroughly in water before handling.

These are the words the other Pyro Bench tools assume you already know. Tap any term to expand it — and jump straight to the tool that uses it.
